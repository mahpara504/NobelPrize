📖 Biography

Sir Alexander Fleming FRS FRSE FRCS was a Scottish physician and microbiologist, best known for discovering the antibiotic substance penicillin in 1928. He shared the 1945 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine with Howard Florey and Ernst Boris Chain, who together developed penicillin into a life-saving drug.

Fleming's discovery came almost by accident: a contaminated petri dish revealed that the mould Penicillium notatum was killing the surrounding bacteria. Recognising the significance of this, he named the active substance "penicillin" and published his findings.

The development of penicillin during World War II saved an estimated 200 million lives by treating bacterial infections that had previously been fatal. Fleming's serendipitous observation is considered one of the most important events in the history of medicine.

🏅 Nobel Prize — Physiology or Medicine, 1945

Fleming was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1945, shared with Howard Florey and Ernst Chain, for the discovery of penicillin — often described as the antibiotic that saved more lives than any other drug in history.
