📖 Biography

Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela was a South African anti-apartheid activist, politician, and statesman who served as the first president of South Africa from 1994 to 1999. He was the country's first black head of state and the first elected in a fully representative democratic election.

Mandela spent 27 years in prison — much of it on Robben Island — before his release in 1990 led to the negotiated end of apartheid. His political philosophy evolved from African nationalism to a broader non-racial humanism, emphasising reconciliation over revenge.

Revered globally as a symbol of freedom and social justice, Mandela received more than 250 honours, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993 (shared with F.W. de Klerk). In South Africa, he is known as "Madiba," a clan name that became a term of endearment worldwide.

🏅 Nobel Prize — Peace, 1993

Mandela was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize jointly with F.W. de Klerk in 1993 for their work in the peaceful transition from apartheid to democracy in South Africa, a transition that was achieved through negotiation rather than civil war.
