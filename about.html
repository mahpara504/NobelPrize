About the Nobel Prize

The world's most prestigious award, recognising outstanding contributions to humanity since 1901.

History

The Legacy of Alfred Nobel

Alfred Bernhard Nobel (1833–1896) was a Swedish chemist, engineer, and inventor best known for inventing dynamite. Troubled by how his inventions were being used for warfare, Nobel signed his last will and testament in 1895, leaving the bulk of his fortune to establish a series of prizes for those who have "conferred the greatest benefit to humankind."

The Nobel Prizes were first awarded on 10 December 1901, the fifth anniversary of Alfred Nobel's death, a date that remains Nobel Prize Day to this day.

Today, the prizes are awarded annually in Stockholm and Oslo, drawing global attention to the scientists, writers, economists, and peace activists who are pushing the boundaries of human understanding.

How It Works

The Selection Process

September — Invitations Sent

The Nobel Committees send out confidential invitations to thousands of scientists, professors, and previous laureates to submit nominations.

February — Deadline

Nomination forms are due. The Nobel Committees compile all valid nominations and begin the deliberation process with expert advisors.

March – August — Review

Committees evaluate candidates, consulting with specialists worldwide. Short lists are drawn up and each candidate's contribution is scrutinised.

October — Announcement Week

Laureates are announced each October, to worldwide attention. The decisions of the awarding institutions are final and cannot be appealed.

10 December — Award Ceremony

On Nobel Day, laureates receive their prize — a gold medal, a diploma, and a monetary award — at ceremonies in Stockholm and Oslo.

Global Impact of the Nobel Prize

Global Recognition

Nobel Laureates are recognised worldwide, elevating their work to international prominence and opening doors for further funding and collaboration.

Awarded discoveries — from antibiotics to relativity — have saved millions of lives, powered technological revolutions, and transformed daily life.
